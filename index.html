Biography

Mama Rouel Judith Ambe was born to Mr. Yohanna Ambe Kakai (Evg/Sgt Major) and Mama Abigail Tazugrati Ambe on January 13, 1936, in Numan Local Government Area, Adamawa state, Nigeria. She was the second child among her siblings; Yosi Ambe, Vedan Ambe, Kanti Ambe, Helen Love Owuna, Felicia Allen, Lawrence Yapaha, Ishaku Ambe, and Hanatu Ambe, all of whom are of blessed memory. Mama Rouel Ambe grew up in Numan and attended Girls Boarding School (CBS) now Boronum. She furthered her education at WTC Maiduguri, Borno state, and subsequently proceeded to Wusasa School of Nursing, Kaduna state. Upon completing her studies, she returned to work with Sudan United Mission Hospital (SUM), Numan. In 1959, she got married to late Mr. Abel Tula Leneke, and they had two children: Mr. Bala Vincent Leneke and the late Mrs. Mildred Ohanete (née Leneke). Mama Rouel Ambe was a seasoned, registered, and accomplished nurse who worked in nearly all departments within the nursing profession, making it a field of envy. After her initial appointment at SUM Hospital in Numan, she advanced her career at Kaduna Teaching Hospital and later at Nursing Home Maiduguri. Another notable position she held was at Chester Infirmary Hospital in the UK. She was the pioneer Principal of the Nursing and Midwifery School in Bauchi. Throughout her career, she held various significant positions, including Executive Secretary of the Women's Commission in Adamawa State, Sole Administrator of Güyük Local Government, Chief Nursing Officer in charge of the Health Services Management Board, Head of the Nursing Department at the Ministry of Health in Adamawa State, State and Regional Manager of the Planned Parenthood Federation of Nigeria, and Adamawa State Chairperson of the Inter-Africa Committee (IAC). She attended numerous seminars and workshops, both locally and internationally, in countries such as the Gambia, USA, UK, Ethiopia, and Ghana, where she presented scientific papers. Amongst her professional qualifications and Seminars conferences attended were;

  • Nigerian Registered Nurse, Wusasa, Zaria 1959
  • Registered Midwife Health School of Hygiene, Ibadan 1964
  • Basic Health Service Scheme certificate – WHO Lagos 1969
  • Public Health Nurse College of Education, Yaba, Lagos 1972
  • Family Planning Certificate – Atlanta, Geogia, USA 1977
  • Post Graduate Diploma in Health Services and Hospital Administration, South Bank Poly, London, UK 1983
  • Certificate of Network Trainer by Pathfinder, Lagos 1986-1987
  • Project Management Diploma – International Health Center, Connecticut, USA 1998
  • International Health Education Conference, London, UK 1986
  • Maxwell Theological Leadership Course (EQUIP) 2008

Mama Rouel received an international award by Pathfinder International Department, on Reproductive Health in the year 2007, Gospel of the Nation Christian Association of Nigeria of commissioning by Christian association of Nigeria (CAN). Mama Rouel was a very jovial, sociable, and cheerful personality. She was indeed a resourceful woman. She was an active member of the local assembly of the LCCN Chapel of Grace, where she belonged to the Women's Fellowship Group before she moved to LCCN Alheri. She was a talented song composer and even wrote a book compiling Christian songs. She was an ardent follower of music and loved singing to the glory of God. Her hobbies were charity works, music (singing), gardening, and housekeeping. Mama Rouel is survived by her son, Bala Vincent Leneke; her adopted daughter, Vera Ambe; her stepmother, Mama Sabina Yohana Ambe; brothers and sisters from her stepmother; cousins, nephews, nieces, grandchildren, in-laws, relations, friends, and well-wishers.

MAY HER BEAUTIFUL SOUL REST IN PEACE WITH THE LORD. AMEN
